01 — The Context

Local Challenges

The Bolívar province of Ecuador is one of the country's poorest regions. 40% of rural people live in poverty. 95% of surrounding communities are indigenous, predominantly Kichwa-speaking, and their culture is under threat from centuries of colonisation and modern globalisation.

Less than 25% of young people progress beyond middle school. Poor agricultural returns force migration to cities. 500 years of colonisation have eroded indigenous language and culture. El Terreno works at the intersection of these challenges, alongside communities as partners — not subjects.

Since launching in July 2022, we have impacted hundreds of lives through over 130 community workshops and events. We work across education, health, water, ecology, culture and economic development — always in genuine partnership with the communities we serve.

Our Approach

We do not arrive with pre-designed solutions. We listen first. Every project begins with deep engagement with community members to understand the real challenges, the cultural context and the existing strengths. Solutions are co-designed, locally owned, and built to outlast our presence.
